what really is the internet?

n simple terms, the internet is a global network of computers and other devices that are connected together, allowing them to communicate and share information with each other. It's like a giant web of interconnected devices that spans the entire world. Imagine the internet as a massive network of roads connecting different cities and towns. Each device connected to the internet, such as computers, smartphones, tablets, and servers, is like a house or building along these roads. When you use the internet, you're essentially sending and receiving data (like messages, pictures, videos, and web pages) between your device and other devices around the world. This data travels through the network of connections, passing through various routers, switches, and servers until it reaches its destination.

Connecting to the internet

  1. Internet Service Provider (ISP) or Mobile Network Provicder

    It typically involves an ISP (Internet Service Provider) in the case of a wired connections, such as DSL, cable, fiber optic, or satellite. They deliver internet connectivity to homes, businesses, and other locations using infrastructure like phone lines, coaxial cables, fiber optic cables, or wireless technologies. Example is Spectranet, ipNX, Starlink e.t.c Mobile Network Providers also known as mobile carriers or wireless carriers, offer wireless cellular network services for mobile devices such as smartphones, tablets, and mobile hotspots. In addition, they offer voice calling, texting (SMS), and multimedia messaging (MMS) services. They operate cellular networks that allow devices to connect to the internet wirelessly using technologies like GSM, CDMA, LTE, and 5G. Examples include MTN, Airtel, Glo, Etisalat
